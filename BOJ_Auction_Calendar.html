Japan fiscal watchlist — key dates for JGB supply and policy

TimingEventRelevance
Early Jul 2026Revenue FY2025 final tax-revenue settlement (MOF)Revenue outturn frames the scale of any autumn supplementary budget.
Aug 31, 2026Budget Ministries' FY2027 budget requests submitted to MOFFirst indication of spending pressure under the Takaichi administration.
Oct–Dec 2026Budget Supplementary budget windowA stimulus package on the scale of the 2025 "Comprehensive Economic Measures" would imply additional JGB issuance and revisions to the auction calendar.
Mid-Dec 2026Tax Ruling-party tax reform outline for FY2027Sets the revenue side of the budget; signals the government's fiscal stance.
Late Dec 2026Issuance Cabinet approval of the FY2027 draft budget and the FY2027 JGB Issuance PlanDetermines gross issuance and the maturity mix — notably whether MOF continues to reduce super-long supply with 30y/40y yields elevated. Coincides with the BOJ's deliberations on its JGB purchase plan from April 2027: the point where the fiscal and monetary calendars intersect.
Jan 2027Diet Ordinary Diet session convenesBudget deliberations begin; political friction can delay passage.
By end-Mar 2027Budget FY2027 budget passageRequired before the fiscal year starts April 1.
Jun 2027Framework Basic Policy on Economic and Fiscal Management ("honebuto")Sets the medium-term fiscal framework, including the treatment of the primary-balance target.
Ad hocMarkets MOF meetings with JGB Market Special Participants and JGB investorsTypically precede changes to the issuance mix; these consultations moved markets during the 2025 super-long selloff.
Ad hocRatings Sovereign rating reviewsSustained stimulus under an expansionary government raises the salience of rating-agency commentary for the long end.
Japan's fiscal year runs April–March. Highlighted rows are the nearest and most market-sensitive items. Dates for recurring events follow the standard annual cycle and are confirmed by MOF/Cabinet Office announcements.
