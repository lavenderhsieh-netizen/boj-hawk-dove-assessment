Key speakers — how their tone changed

UEDA Kazuo Hawkish drift
Governor
Patient normalizer → confident normalizer (≈ +0.5 → +1)
"The Bank... will continue to raise the policy interest rate and adjust the degree of monetary accommodation." — Keidanren, Dec 25, 2025, a week after ending the 11-month tariff pause. Achievement of the 2% target "is steadily approaching."
TAMURA Naoki Strong hawk
Policy Board Member
Consistently the board's most hawkish voice (≈ +2 throughout)
"It is quite possible that, as early as this spring, the price stability target... can be judged to have been achieved"; hike impact so far "extremely limited"; neutral rate "at least around 1 percent." — Kanagawa, Feb 13, 2026. Has since argued for moving toward a ~2% neutral.
TAKATA Hajime Escalating hawk
Policy Board Member
"Gear shift" advocate → proposed 1.0% hike at Jan 2026 MPM (≈ +1.5 → +2)
"At the January 2026 MPM, I proposed that the Bank raise the policy interest rate to 1.0 percent as a further gear shift"; urged "communication that assumes that the price stability target is almost achieved." — Kyoto, Feb 26, 2026.
HIMINO Ryozo Measured hawk
Deputy Governor
Steady gradualist; intellectual groundwork for hikes (≈ +0.5 → +1)
Jan 2025: "An Economy with Positive Interest Rates" preceded the Jan hike. Mar 2026: policy "should gradually shift to a more neutral stance through moderate policy rate hikes."
UCHIDA Shinichi Dove → neutral
Deputy Governor
Aug 2024 market-calmer → on-consensus (≈ −1 → 0)
After the Aug 2024 market rout, pledged not to hike amid instability — the board's dovish anchor. By Jul 2025 (Kochi): standard guidance to keep raising rates if the outlook is realized. Fronted the June 2026 presser during Ueda's hospitalization.
NOGUCHI Asahi Softening dove
Policy Board Member
Reflationist dove → reluctant gradualist (≈ −1.5 → −0.5)
"The most realistic approach... is to conduct policy interest rate hikes incrementally"; a pace that is "too slow... increases the risk of destabilizing economic activity and prices." — Oita, Nov 27, 2025. A notable concession from the board's leading dove.
NAKAGAWA Junko Centrist, late hawkish tilt
Policy Board Member (term ended Jun 2026)
On-consensus throughout → reportedly called for a hike in Apr 2026 (≈ 0 → +0.5)
"The Bank will continue to raise the policy interest rate... in response to improvement in economic and price developments." — Okayama, Nov 10, 2025. Succeeded by Sato from July 2026.
KOEDA Junko New normalizer
Policy Board Member (from Apr 2025, replaced dove Adachi)
Data-driven, leans hawkish from arrival (≈ +1)
"Underlying inflation is about 2 percent"; "the Bank needs to proceed with interest rate normalization... to avoid creating unintended distortions." — Niigata, Nov 20, 2025.
MASU Kazuyuki Cautious normalizer
Policy Board Member (from mid-2025, replaced dove Nakamura)
Supports normalization, stresses caution (≈ +0.5)
"I am convinced that continuing with further policy interest rate hikes will be needed to complete the normalization"... but "the Bank will therefore proceed cautiously with rate hikes." — Ehime, Feb 6, 2026.
ASADA Toichiro New dove
Policy Board Member (2026; PM Takaichi's first BOJ pick)
Sole dissenter at the June 2026 hike (≈ −1.5)
Voted to hold at 0.75% at only his second meeting, citing downside risks to production and employment — the clearest new dovish counterweight on the board.
Departed doves Board turnover
NAKAMURA Toyoaki (to Jun 2025) · ADACHI Seiji (to Mar 2025)
Their exits shifted the board's center of gravity hawkish
Nakamura repeatedly warned against premature tightening; Adachi urged patience. Their seats went to Masu and Koeda, both of whom back continued normalization.

Timeline of key events

Jul 31, 2024
Rate raised to 0.25%; JGB purchase-reduction plan announced.
Aug 5–7, 2024
Global market rout; Deputy Governor Uchida calms markets — no hikes while markets are unstable.
Jan 24, 2025
Rate raised to 0.5% (highest since 2008), after Himino's and Ueda's signaling.
Apr 2025
US tariff shock: Outlook Report cuts FY25 growth forecast from 1.1% to 0.5%; hiking cycle pauses.
Jun–Sep 2025
JGB taper extended at gentler pace from Apr 2026; ETF/J-REIT sales decided (Sep). Jul: Japan–US trade agreement reduces uncertainty.
Sep & Oct 2025
Holds, but Tamura and Takata dissent at both meetings, proposing 0.75% — the hawks force the debate.
Oct 2025
Takaichi government takes office; Oct Outlook: FY25 CPI 2.7%, FY26 1.8%, FY27 2.0%.
Dec 19, 2025
Rate raised to 0.75% (unanimous) — first hike in 11 months; 10-year JGB yield tops 2%.
Jan 2026
Hold, but Takata proposes going to 1.0%. Jan Outlook restores pre-tariff forecasts.
Apr 28, 2026
Hold at 0.75% amid Iran-war worries; inflation forecast raised. Nakagawa reportedly calls for a hike.
Jun 16, 2026
Rate raised to 1.00% (7–1; Asada dissents), highest since Sep 1995, amid yen weakness and large FX intervention. Uchida fronts the press conference with Ueda hospitalized.
Method. Scores are qualitative judgments (−2 strong dove … +2 strong hawk) assigned per half-year from each speaker's official BOJ speeches (boj.or.jp "Speeches and Statements", 2024–2026 listings), MPM proposals and dissents cited in those speeches, and Outlook Report framing; corroborated with press coverage for events after the latest speeches. Primary sources include: Tamura (Feb 13, 2026, Kanagawa), Takata (Feb 26, 2026, Kyoto), Ueda (Dec 25, 2025, Keidanren), Noguchi (Nov 27, 2025, Oita), Nakagawa (Nov 10, 2025, Okayama), Koeda (Nov 20, 2025, Niigata), Masu (Feb 6, 2026, Ehime), Himino (Mar 2, 2026, Wakayama), Uchida (Jul 23, 2025, Kochi). News: CNBC (Dec 19, 2025; Jun 16, 2026), Japan Times (Jun 2026), Bloomberg (Jun 21, 2026); voting records from BOJ policy statements and press reports (Reuters/Investing.com on Sep–Oct 2025 dissents; investingLive Dec 29, 2025 on the unanimous December vote; Nippon.com/JIJI Apr 1, 2026 on Asada's appointment). Tenure dates per BOJ Policy Board announcements. This is an analytical assessment, not investment advice.
